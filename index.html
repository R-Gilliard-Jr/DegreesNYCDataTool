Introduction and Instructions

The graphs throughout this application are intended to display the paths students take to and through college, beginning in their freshman year of high school. Each of the tabs contains a bar graph which can be be updated to show students in different 9th grade cohorts, of different race/ethnicity, gender, from different Boroughs, and with different education statuses, among other characteristics. The following dropdown menus and sliders appear on one or more of the tabs:

  • Primary Characteristic: Users can select a primary characteristic, which will change the rows that appear on the graph.
  • Secondary Characteristic: Users can select a secondary characteristic. Change the options available in the subgroup dropdown menu (discussed below) and will update the graph to contain only data for the default subgroup.
  • Subgroup: Users can select a subset of the secondary characteristic. For example, if the secondary characteristic is "Gender", users can select either "Male" or "Female".
  • High School Graduation: Users can select the timing of high school graduation: ever, on-time, or late.
  • College Enrollment Level: Users can select the college enrollment level of interest: any, two year, or four year college.
  • Years Persisted: Users can select the number of years of persistence of interest: two or three.
  • Cohort: Users can adjust the cohort of interest. The years represent the year that students in the cohort started ninth grade.

Dos and Don'ts with the Data

The chart(s) throughout this application present aggregated high school data for students who attended school in the NYCDOE. The data can be broken up along many different dimensions: race/ethnicity, gender, English language learners, individualized education program status, and free/reduced price lunch eligibility. The best data is not without bias, and further bias is added as data is interpreted. Here are some dos and dont's to consider as you explore the chart(s) on this page.
  • Do: Remember that, though these groups are presented separately here, they are not independent phenomena in reality. Students can have more than one of these identities and the consequences of discrimination mean that students experiencing one form of marginalization are likely to experience others.
  • Don't: Neglect the the impact of discrimination on student outcomes. As differences emerge in these charts, consider what barriers students in different groups are exposed to that prevent them from reaching their full potential. Avoid overly simplistic conclusions about student ability.

Definitions

  • Race - Student, self-selected race/ethnicity. Limited to the values of Asian, Black, Latinx, White, and Other.
  • Gender - Student, self-selected gender. Limited to the, binary, male and female.
  • English Language Learner - Students whose native language is not english and who have been identified as in need of additional support in learning English.
  • Individualized Education Program - Students with an individualized education program, developed by their school in partnership with the student and their guardian(s).
  • Free/reduced Price Lunch - Students who are eligible for free/reduced price lunch.
  • Borough - The borough of New York City in which the student attended high school.
  • Cohort - 9th grade cohort. The year shown represents the year that students begin 9th grade.
  • On-track - Defined as completion of 10 or more credits and passing one regents exam by the end of 9th grade.
  • High School Graduation - Timing of high school graduation. On-time indicates that a student graduated within 4 years of enrolling; late indicates that a student did not. High school graduation only includes students who graduated with a Regents or Local diploma. Students graduating with a GED or IEP diploma are counted as non-graduates.
  • College Enrollment Level - Level of college at which students enrolled. Choices are limited to any, 2 year college, and 4 year college.
  • Years Persisted - Number of years that a student successfully re-enrolled in college. The denominator for figures in this tab is students who enrolled in college, rather than the full cohort.
